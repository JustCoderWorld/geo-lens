How to get cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity in 2026 (8 proven tactics)

By GEO Lens · June 28, 2026

Getting cited by AI answer engines is now a distinct discipline from ranking on Google. AI engines look for consensus across independent sources, clear structure and original information. Here are eight tactics that work in 2026 — and why they matter (brands cited in AI results see up to 3.2× higher conversion, and AI referral traffic converts ~4.4× better than traditional organic).

1. Build a multi-source consensus

AI systems gain confidence when your product appears with consistent messaging across Reddit, YouTube, review sites and your own site. Reddit alone accounts for roughly 46% of Perplexity's citations — be genuinely present where your audience talks.

2. Publish original data

Pages with a statistic that exists nowhere else get cited at nearly 3× the rate of pages that recycle industry figures. Run a small study, share a data point from your own product, and become a primary source.

3. Add structured data (schema)

FAQPage and Article JSON-LD make your content machine-readable and individual answers directly citable. Generate it free with our FAQPage schema generator.

4. Lead with the answer

Put a concise, self-contained answer at the top of each section — that is the exact text AI tends to quote.

5. Stay fresh

Perplexity favors content published or updated in the last 12 months. Show a visible date and keep pages current.

6. Use question-based headings

Match the phrasing of real user prompts ("How do I…?") so engines map your section to the query.

7. Show trust signals (E-E-A-T)

Name the author, cite reputable sources, and be specific. Vague content does not get cited; specific, data-backed content does.

8. Measure citability, not just rankings

Your keyword tools can't see whether AI can parse your page. GEO Lens scores any page's AI citability across all the signals above and generates the fixes in one click.
