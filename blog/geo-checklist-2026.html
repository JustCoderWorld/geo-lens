The 2026 GEO checklist: 14 signals AI answer engines reward

By GEO Lens · June 28, 2026

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of structuring content so AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity and Google AI Overviews can understand, trust and cite it. This is the practical 14-point checklist we use — and the one GEO Lens scores automatically on any page.

The checklist

  1. Structured data (schema.org). Add JSON-LD — especially FAQPage on Q&A content. AI engines lean on schema to parse meaning.
  2. Question-based headings. Phrase H2/H3s as the questions users actually ask AI ("How does X work?").
  3. FAQ / Q&A content. A real FAQ block is prime citation material.
  4. Answer-first opening. Lead each section with a concise, self-contained answer AI can quote directly.
  5. Lists and tables. Scannable, extractable structure beats walls of text.
  6. Clear definitions. Plain "X is a …" sentences help entity understanding.
  7. Named author. Show who wrote it (E-E-A-T).
  8. Outbound citations. Link 2+ reputable sources; AI trusts well-sourced pages.
  9. Freshness. Show a visible, recent updated date — Perplexity favors content from the last 12 months.
  10. Content depth. Specific, data-backed depth gets cited; vague content does not.
  11. Single clear H1. State the page topic plainly, once.
  12. Descriptive title (≈15–65 chars). Clear and answer-oriented.
  13. Meta description (≈50–160 chars). Directly answer the page's core question.
  14. Image alt text. Give AI context for your visuals.

FAQ

What is a GEO checklist?

A GEO checklist is the set of on-page signals that make content easy for AI answer engines to understand, trust and cite.

Is GEO different from SEO?

Yes — SEO optimizes for ranking; GEO optimizes for being cited inside AI answers. In 2026 the two overlap far less than they used to.
